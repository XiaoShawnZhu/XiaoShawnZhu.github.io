Introduction

 

I am a third-year Ph.D. student in Computer Science and Engineering at the University of Michigan, advised by Professor Z. Morley Mao. Before joining Michigan, I received my B.S. degree in Electronic Engineering from Tsinghua University in 2016. During my undergraduate study, I spent one semester as an exchange student in School of Computing at National University of Singapore.

My research interests lie in the areas of computer networking, mobile computing, and security, particularly in investigating how network protocols and architectures can impact application performance on end hosts. I discover real-world performance, deployability and security problems of networked computer systems by developing novel analysis methodologies and build practical systems to address the challenges of tackling them.

My on-going and past research topics include multi-path transport protocols, cooperative mobile networking, smart wearable systems, millimeter-wave networking, TCP congestion control, software-defined networking, cyber-physical systems security, and mobile edge computing.
